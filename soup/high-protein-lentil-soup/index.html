High Protein Lentil Soup

By Recipe Team · Published 2026-07-17 · 26g protein per serving

Lentil soup rarely gets top billing, but a pot of brown lentils simmered with vegetables and warm spices quietly delivers more protein per serving than most people expect from a fully vegetarian soup.

It's the kind of recipe that scales up easily and tastes better the next day, which makes it one of the more practical meal-prep options on the site — not just a dinner, but four or five lunches.

High Protein Lentil Soup

Prep: 15 min Cook: 25 min Total: 40 min Yield: 6 servings 280 cal · 26g protein

Ingredients

  • 2 cups dried brown lentils, rinsed
  • 2 carrots, diced
  • 2 celery stalks, diced
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 can diced tomatoes
  • 6 cups vegetable broth
  • 1 tsp cumin
  • 1 tsp smoked paprika
  • 1 bay leaf
  • 2 tbsp olive oil
  • 2 tbsp lemon juice
  • Fresh parsley, for serving

Instructions

  1. 1. Sauté the vegetables

    Heat olive oil in a large pot over medium heat. Add onion, carrots, and celery, cooking for 6-7 minutes until softened. Add garlic, cumin, and smoked paprika, cooking for 1 more minute.

  2. 2. Add lentils and liquid

    Stir in rinsed lentils, diced tomatoes, vegetable broth, and bay leaf. Bring to a boil, then reduce heat and simmer uncovered for 25-30 minutes, until the lentils are tender.

  3. 3. Finish the soup

    Remove the bay leaf. Stir in lemon juice, and season with salt and pepper to taste.

  4. 4. Serve

    Ladle into bowls and top with fresh parsley. Serve with crusty bread if desired.

Tips & Common Questions

Why use brown lentils instead of red?

Brown lentils hold their shape through a longer simmer, giving this soup a heartier, more textured bite than red lentils, which break down into a smoother, thicker consistency.

Can I make this soup in a slow cooker?

Yes — combine all ingredients except the lemon juice and cook on low for 6-7 hours or high for 3-4 hours, then stir in lemon juice just before serving.

Why add lemon juice at the end?

A splash of acid at the end brightens the whole pot and keeps the soup from tasting flat — lentil soups in particular benefit from this final adjustment.

How long does this soup keep, and does it freeze well?

It keeps up to 5 days in the fridge and freezes well for up to 3 months — the flavor actually improves after a day or two in the fridge as the spices settle in.
