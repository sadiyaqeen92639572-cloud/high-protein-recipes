High Protein Crockpot Chicken Chili

By Recipe Team · Published 2026-07-17 · 40g protein per serving

White chicken chili is the crockpot recipe that quietly does more work than it looks like — dump everything in before work, and six hours later there's a pot of shredded chicken chili with almost no active cooking time.

At 40g of protein per serving, it's one of the highest-protein crockpot meals that doesn't rely on a protein powder shortcut — just chicken, beans, and time.

High Protein Crockpot Chicken Chili

Prep: 15 min Cook: 6 hr Total: 6 hr 15 min Yield: 6 servings 360 cal · 40g protein

Ingredients

  • 2 lbs boneless, skinless chicken breast
  • 3 cans white beans, drained and rinsed
  • 2 cans diced green chiles
  • 4 cups chicken broth
  • 1 onion, diced
  • 3 cloves garlic, minced
  • 1 tbsp cumin
  • 1 tsp oregano
  • 1/2 tsp cayenne (optional)
  • 1/2 cup plain Greek yogurt, for serving
  • Shredded cheese and cilantro, for serving

Instructions

  1. 1. Load the crockpot

    Add chicken breast, white beans, green chiles, chicken broth, onion, garlic, cumin, oregano, and cayenne (if using) to the crockpot. Stir to combine.

  2. 2. Slow cook

    Cover and cook on low for 6 hours, or on high for 3-4 hours, until the chicken shreds easily with a fork.

  3. 3. Shred the chicken

    Remove the chicken breasts, shred with two forks, and return to the crockpot. Stir to combine with the broth and beans.

  4. 4. Finish and serve

    Ladle into bowls and top each with a dollop of Greek yogurt, shredded cheese, and fresh cilantro.

Tips & Common Questions

Can I use chicken thighs instead of breast?

Yes — boneless, skinless thighs work well and stay slightly more moist, though the protein content per serving will be a touch lower since thighs have more fat.

Why add Greek yogurt instead of sour cream?

Greek yogurt adds nearly the same creamy tang as sour cream but with significantly more protein per tablespoon — a simple swap that boosts the final protein count.

Can I make this on the stovetop instead?

Yes — simmer everything in a large pot over low heat for about 45 minutes, covered, until the chicken is fully cooked and shreds easily.

How long does this keep in the fridge?

Up to 5 days in an airtight container, and it freezes well for up to 3 months — freeze in individual portions for easy grab-and-go lunches.
