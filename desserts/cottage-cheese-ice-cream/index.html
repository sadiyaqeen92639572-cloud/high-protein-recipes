Cottage Cheese Ice Cream

By Recipe Team · Published 2026-07-11 · 18g protein per serving

Some mornings call for pancakes that actually keep you full past 10am. These cottage cheese pancakes blend straight in the blender — no protein powder, no chalky aftertaste — and pack 18g of protein into a stack of fluffy, golden pancakes.

The trick is blending the cottage cheese completely smooth so it disappears into the batter, leaving behind moisture and protein without any curdy texture. Ready in 15 minutes, and the batter keeps overnight if you want to prep ahead.

Cottage Cheese Ice Cream

Prep: 5 min Cook: 10 min Total: 15 min Yield: 4 servings 150 cal · 18g protein

Ingredients

  • 2 cups full-fat cottage cheese
  • 1/3 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tbsp vanilla extract
  • Pinch of salt
  • Optional mix-ins: chocolate chips, fresh berries, crushed graham crackers

Instructions

  1. 1. Blend until completely smooth

    Add cottage cheese, honey, vanilla, and salt to a blender or food processor. Blend on high for 1-2 minutes, scraping down the sides, until totally smooth with zero curd texture remaining.

  2. 2. Fold in mix-ins

    If using, fold in chocolate chips or crushed graham crackers by hand — don't blend them in.

  3. 3. Freeze

    Pour into a loaf pan or airtight container. Freeze for at least 4 hours, or until firm.

  4. 4. Scoop and serve

    Let sit at room temperature for 5-10 minutes before scooping for the best soft-serve texture.

Tips & Common Questions

Why is my cottage cheese ice cream icy instead of creamy?

Under-blending is the most common cause — the mixture needs to be 100% smooth with no visible curds before freezing, or you'll get an icy texture instead of creamy. Blend longer than you think you need to.

Do I need an ice cream maker?

No — this is a no-churn recipe. The honey and full-fat cottage cheese keep it soft enough to scoop straight from the freezer without an ice cream maker.

Can I use low-fat cottage cheese?

You can, but full-fat gives a noticeably creamier result. Low-fat versions freeze slightly harder and icier.

How long does it keep in the freezer?

Up to 2 weeks in an airtight container. Texture is best in the first week.
