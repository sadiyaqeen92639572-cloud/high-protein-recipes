Cottage Cheese Pancakes

By Recipe Team · Published 2026-07-11 · 24g protein per serving

Some mornings call for pancakes that actually keep you full past 10am. These cottage cheese pancakes blend straight in the blender — no protein powder, no chalky aftertaste — and pack 24g of protein into a stack of fluffy, golden pancakes.

The trick is blending the cottage cheese completely smooth so it disappears into the batter, leaving behind moisture and protein without any curdy texture. Ready in 15 minutes, and the batter keeps overnight if you want to prep ahead.

A quick note on texture: don't skip the 2-3 minute rest after blending. The flour needs a moment to hydrate, and that's what gives you pancakes that hold together on the flip instead of falling apart.

Cottage Cheese Pancakes

Prep: 5 min Cook: 10 min Total: 15 min Yield: 2 servings (about 8 pancakes) 310 cal · 24g protein

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cottage cheese (full-fat or low-fat)
  • 4 large eggs
  • 1/2 cup all-purpose flour (or oat flour for gluten-free)
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 tsp baking powder
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Butter or oil, for the pan

Instructions

  1. 1. Blend the batter

    Add cottage cheese, eggs, flour, honey, baking powder, salt, and vanilla to a blender. Blend until completely smooth, about 30 seconds.

  2. 2. Rest the batter

    Let the batter sit for 2-3 minutes — this helps the flour hydrate and gives fluffier pancakes.

  3. 3. Heat the pan

    Heat a lightly buttered non-stick skillet over medium-low heat.

  4. 4. Cook the pancakes

    Pour 1/4 cup of batter per pancake. Cook 2-3 minutes until bubbles form on the surface, then flip and cook 1-2 minutes more until golden.

  5. 5. Serve

    Stack and top with fresh berries, a drizzle of honey or maple syrup, and a dusting of powdered sugar if desired.

Tips & Common Questions

Can I make the batter ahead of time?

Yes — the batter keeps in the fridge for up to 24 hours. Give it a quick stir before cooking since it thickens slightly as it sits.

Can I freeze cottage cheese pancakes?

Yes. Cool completely, layer with parchment paper, and freeze in an airtight bag for up to 2 months. Reheat in a toaster or skillet.

Why blend the cottage cheese instead of leaving it chunky?

Blending breaks down the curds completely, so you get a smooth, fluffy pancake texture instead of pockets of cottage cheese — most readers prefer this, but you can pulse briefly for a few small curds if you like texture.

Can I substitute the flour for a gluten-free option?

Yes — oat flour or a 1:1 gluten-free blend both work well without changing the ratios.
