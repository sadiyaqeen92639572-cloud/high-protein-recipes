Protein Overnight Oats

By Recipe Team · Published 2026-07-11 · 28g protein per serving

Some mornings call for pancakes that actually keep you full past 10am. These cottage cheese pancakes blend straight in the blender — no protein powder, no chalky aftertaste — and pack 28g of protein into a stack of fluffy, golden pancakes.

The trick is blending the cottage cheese completely smooth so it disappears into the batter, leaving behind moisture and protein without any curdy texture. Ready in 15 minutes, and the batter keeps overnight if you want to prep ahead.

Protein Overnight Oats

Prep: 5 min Cook: 10 min Total: 15 min Yield: 1 serving (1 jar) 380 cal · 28g protein

Ingredients

  • 1/2 cup rolled oats
  • 1/4 cup cottage cheese
  • 1/2 cup Greek yogurt
  • 1/2 cup milk (dairy or unsweetened almond milk)
  • 1 tbsp chia seeds
  • 1 tbsp honey or maple syrup
  • 1/2 tsp vanilla extract
  • Toppings: sliced banana, peanut butter, walnuts

Instructions

  1. 1. Blend the creamy base

    Add cottage cheese, Greek yogurt, milk, honey, and vanilla to a blender. Blend until completely smooth, about 20 seconds.

  2. 2. Combine with oats

    Pour the blended mixture into a jar with the rolled oats and chia seeds. Stir well to combine.

  3. 3. Refrigerate overnight

    Cover and refrigerate for at least 8 hours, or overnight. The oats will absorb the liquid and turn thick and creamy.

  4. 4. Top and serve

    In the morning, stir once more and top with sliced banana, a swirl of peanut butter, and walnuts.

Tips & Common Questions

Can I use protein powder instead of cottage cheese?

You can, but the texture and protein content here come from whole-food ingredients (cottage cheese + Greek yogurt), which most readers find creamier and less chalky than powder-based versions.

How long do protein overnight oats keep in the fridge?

Up to 3-4 days in a sealed jar. Add fresh toppings just before eating rather than in advance.

Can I make this dairy-free?

Swap the cottage cheese and Greek yogurt for a dairy-free yogurt alternative and unsweetened almond or oat milk — the protein content will be lower without the dairy base.

Why blend the wet ingredients instead of just stirring?

Blending breaks down the cottage cheese curds completely, so the oats end up smooth and creamy instead of having pockets of cottage cheese texture.
