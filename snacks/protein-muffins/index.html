Protein Muffins with Cottage Cheese

By Recipe Team · Published 2026-07-12 · 12g protein per serving

Most muffin recipes get their protein from a scoop of powder stirred in as an afterthought. This one builds it into the batter itself — cottage cheese blended smooth with the eggs before the dry ingredients ever get involved.

12g of protein per muffin, 30 minutes start to finish, and a texture soft enough that nobody would guess cottage cheese is in there at all.

Jump to Recipe ↓

Stop mixing the moment the dry ingredients disappear into the batter. Every extra stir past that point develops more gluten in the oat flour, which is what makes muffins dense instead of tender.

Protein Muffins with Cottage Cheese

Prep: 10 min Cook: 20 min Total: 30 min Yield: 12 muffins 150 cal · 12g protein

Ingredients

  • 1 cup cottage cheese
  • 3 large eggs
  • 1/3 cup honey or maple syrup
  • 1 tsp vanilla extract
  • 1 3/4 cups oat flour
  • 1 tsp baking powder
  • 1/2 tsp baking soda
  • 1/4 tsp salt
  • 1/2 cup chocolate chips or blueberries (optional)

Instructions

  1. 1. Blend the wet ingredients

    Blend cottage cheese, eggs, honey, and vanilla until completely smooth, about 30 seconds.

  2. 2. Combine with dry ingredients

    Whisk oat flour, baking powder, baking soda, and salt in a bowl. Pour in the wet mixture and stir until just combined. Fold in chocolate chips or blueberries if using.

  3. 3. Fill and bake

    Divide batter between 12 lined muffin cups. Bake at 350°F for 18-20 minutes, until a toothpick comes out clean.

  4. 4. Cool

    Let muffins cool in the pan for 5 minutes before transferring to a wire rack.

Tips & Common Questions

Can I use all-purpose flour instead of oat flour?

Yes, 1:1 by volume — the texture will be slightly lighter and less dense than with oat flour.

Why blend the cottage cheese instead of leaving it chunky?

A full blend is what gives these muffins a soft, uniform crumb — unblended cottage cheese leaves visible curds throughout the batter.

How do I store protein muffins?

Up to 4 days at room temperature in an airtight container, or freeze for up to 2 months — thaw at room temperature or microwave for 20 seconds.

Why did my muffins turn out dense?

Overmixing the batter after adding the dry ingredients is the most common cause — stir just until no dry flour streaks remain, then stop.
