High Protein Cottage Cheese Dip

By Recipe Team · Published 2026-07-11 · 14g protein per serving

Whipped cottage cheese is one of those ideas that sounds strange until you taste it — a full blend turns it into something closer to a lighter, tangier cream cheese than anything resembling curds.

This version leans savory, with everything bagel seasoning and a squeeze of lemon, and comes together in five minutes with zero cooking. 14g of protein per serving, and it disappears fast next to a plate of cut vegetables.

High Protein Cottage Cheese Dip

Prep: 5 min Cook: 0 min Total: 5 min Yield: 4 servings 90 cal · 14g protein

Ingredients

  • 2 cups cottage cheese
  • 1 tbsp olive oil
  • 1 tbsp lemon juice
  • 1 tsp everything bagel seasoning, plus more for topping
  • 2 tbsp chopped chives
  • Salt to taste
  • Sliced cucumber and bell pepper, for serving

Instructions

  1. 1. Blend until smooth

    Add cottage cheese, olive oil, lemon juice, everything bagel seasoning, and salt to a blender or food processor. Blend for 1-2 minutes until completely smooth and airy, scraping down the sides as needed.

  2. 2. Transfer to a bowl

    Spoon the whipped mixture into a serving bowl, swirling the top with the back of a spoon.

  3. 3. Top and serve

    Sprinkle with extra everything bagel seasoning and chopped chives. Serve with sliced cucumber and bell pepper for dipping.

Tips & Common Questions

Why blend the cottage cheese instead of just stirring it?

Blending is what makes this a dip instead of a bowl of cottage cheese with toppings — a full 1-2 minutes in a blender or food processor gives it a whipped, airy texture closer to cream cheese than to curds.

How long does this dip keep in the fridge?

Up to 4 days in an airtight container. The texture stays smooth; just give it a quick stir before serving.

What else can I dip in this besides vegetables?

Whole grain crackers, pita chips, or even as a spread on toast all work well.

Can I make this dip savory in a different way?

Yes — swap the everything bagel seasoning for garlic powder and fresh herbs, or a spoonful of pesto, for a different flavor direction.
